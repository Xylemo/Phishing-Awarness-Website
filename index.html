What Is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyberattack that targets individuals via text, email, phone, or websites to obtain sensitive information. Most of these scams work because they feel urgent, familiar, and take only one click to fall for. Attackers have no need to get past complex firewalls; they only need to get past unsuspecting people. While things like multi-factor authentication reduce the risk of being scammed, phishing can also target 2FA using real-time relay tools to obtain information such as one-time passwords.
